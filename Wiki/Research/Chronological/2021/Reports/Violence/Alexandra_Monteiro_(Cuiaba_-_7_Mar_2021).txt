A 30-year-old transvestite identified as Alexandra was found dead on Sunday (07) by her own mother, in the residence where she lived in the Altos do Cuiabá neighborhood, in the capital.

According to the information, the victim's mother said she spoke to her daughter last Friday (05), however, as the daughter did not answer her anymore and was missing all Saturday (06).

Alexandra's mother went to the property after her daughter and found the transvestite dead in a state of decay. The body was wrapped in a sheet, with hands tied by rope. Family members reported that Alexandra had a troubled relationship with a boy who has already been identified by the police.

They also said that he had been threatening her with death. Relatives missed home appliances and also the victim's cell phone. Case is investigated by DHPP.



Trans woman found dead inside bathroom and police investigating crime in Cuiabá
Family members went to the residence, broke down the door and found the victim dead.
By G1 MT and TV Centro América

03/08/2021 10h02  Updated 18 hours ago

Trans Alexandra Monteiro, 30, was found dead in Cuiabá - Photo: Personal archive

A transsexual woman was found dead in the bathroom of her home on Sunday (7), in Cuiabá. According to the Homicide and Personal Protection Police (DHPP), Alexandra Monteiro, 30, has not been in contact with her family since Friday (5). Family members went to the residence, broke down the door and found the victim dead.

The crime occurred in the Altos neighborhood of Cuiabá and is initially treated as homicide.

According to DHPP, the body was in the bathroom in a state of decay.

The family reported that Alexandra was in a troubled relationship with a person. The case is investigated by DHPP. No suspects were arrested until Monday (8).

Mato Grosso recorded 160 occurrences of crimes against the LGBT public between January and August 2020, according to data from the State Group to Combat Homophobia Crimes (GECCH) released by the State Department of Public Security (Sesp-MT).

Of the total records, four are homicides, four are suicides and two are still to be clarified.

Compared to the same period in 2019 - which had 77 newsletters - the number increased by 108%.
