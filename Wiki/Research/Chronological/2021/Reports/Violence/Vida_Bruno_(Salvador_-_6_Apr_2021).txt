LGBT activist Vida Bruno dies in Salvador

This Tuesday, 6th, the historian and coordinator of Policies and Promotion of LGBTI + Citizenship of the City of Salvador, Vida Bruno, died. According to information from the Gay Group of Bahia (GGB), he spent two months in the ICU of the Teresa de Lisieux Hospital, however, the cause of death was not disclosed by the family.

In November last year, Vida Bruno said he was hit with a blow to the head and several parts of his body in a bar and restaurant in Campo Grande, Salvador. At the time, the Civil Police reported that the aggression had not been coming during the investigations.

In a note, the Salvador City Hall mourned the death of the historian and sympathized with the family. A trans man, Vida Bruno actively fought for human rights, being one of the founders of the LGBT Reference Center , linked to the Municipal Secretariat of Reparation (Semur). He was in charge of the service coordination since the beginning of activities, in 2016.

"Bruno was also one of the main municipal collaborators in the elaboration of public policies for the LGBTI + population in the capital of Bahia," said the agency.
