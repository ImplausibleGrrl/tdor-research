On Thursday, July 1, Ivonne , a 32-year-old trans woman , was murdered in her own barber shop , in the Santa María Aztahuacán neighborhood , Iztapalapa mayor's office, and today, the main suspect is detained and in prison.

Investigative Police (PDI) agents arrested him based on an arrest warrant , accused of causing the death of said person from the LGBTTTI + community .

They killed her in her own business
The capital's District Attorney stressed that the investigation folder indicates that the victim was staying in the establishment with the suspect , who possibly physically assaulted her to the point of causing her death.

The investigations indicate that the individual had fled the place where he committed the crime in the woman's vehicle.

The following day, the sister requested police support, through a help button, and stated that she had no news of Ivonne's whereabouts .

The body of the trans girl was located inside the barbershop , where the aggressor possibly committed the crime.

Is already in the prison
Interviews with people close to him made it possible to establish that Estanislao "N" had been with the victim on the day and time of the events.

These indications motivated the agent of the Public Ministry to request and obtain from a control judge the respective arrest warrant .

The court order was executed outside the North Men's Preventive Prison. 

While the implicated was transferred to the East Men's Preventive Prison, where he was at the disposal of the control judge who requires it.


POLICE / FRIDAY JULY 2, 2021

They find lifeless and violated a trans woman inside a "Barber Shop", in Iztapalapa
The victim named Ivonne "N" was dedicated to sex service at night and that day she would see a man for a service

A transsexual woman was found dead and with signs of torture inside a "Barber Shop" that is located in the Iztapalapa mayor's office; Two 18-year-olds are detained for this crime.

According to the official report around 2:00 p.m. this Thursday, the owner of the business arrived at the establishment and when he saw that it was closed, he called a locksmith to help him open the premises.

Upon entering, the man located the body of the 33-year-old victim named Ivonne "N", who was tied hand and foot. Authorities from Mexico City arrived at the crime scene and certified the death of the transsexual woman, who was an employee of the hairdresser.

Intense confrontation between security elements and armed men in the State of Mexico
A friend of Ivonne's told the authorities that the victim was engaged in sex service at night and that that day she would see a man for a service.

Hours later, elements of the Ministry of Citizen Security (SSC) in the capital managed to arrest two 18-year-olds identified as Nicolás and Jesús for allegedly being related to the femicide.

The pair of suspects were put at the disposal of the Public Ministry of the Narcomenudeo Prosecutor's Office, because at the time of their arrest they were located various doses of drugs.
