> The death of Sophie Vásquez, a transgender Hispanic, has generated concern among those who advocate for greater protection and respect for members of this community.
> 
> The woman was shot to death in events that occurred in the city of Brookhaven, Georgia, on May 4, without the authorities having given further information about the perpetrator or perpetrators of the crime.
> 
> Sophie Vásquez was 36 years old and was part of the “EsTr Community (El / La)” that defends the interests of the trans community in Atlanta so that they can achieve the recognition and access to social services that other groups enjoy.
> 
> The death of Sophie Vásquez joins the 22 that have already taken place so far this year in the country, presaging that it will exceed the figure presented in 2020 where 44 violent deaths were counted against members of this community.
> 
> The “EsTr (El / La) Community” has organized a vigil for the next May 12 in honor of Sophie at the Northeast Plaza in the city of Bookhaven and has established an account on the gofund.me platform in order to help with your funeral expenses.

> Through communications with the Community Estrella and the Brookhaven Police Department, I confirmed reports that a Latina transgender woman, Sophie Vasquez, was murdered in Gwinnett County on the morning of May 4.  She was misgendered and dead named in the police report.   I will keep everyone posted on further developments and any plans regarding a vigil or memorial service.


> Rest in Peace Sophie Vasquez. The 22nd transgender killed this year to transphobic violence. The second trans woman killed in my city.
