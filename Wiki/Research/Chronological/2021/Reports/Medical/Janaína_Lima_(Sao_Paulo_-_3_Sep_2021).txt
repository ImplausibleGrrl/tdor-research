> This Friday (3), in São Paulo, the transvestite activist Janaína Lima died. On Instagram, Professor Dr. Dimitri Nascimento Sales, lamented the passing of the northeastern woman who was born in Rio Grande do Norte and registered in Paraíba who arrived in São Paulo as a child. “The news of Janaina Lima's death paralyzed me. The days have been so difficult, losing loved ones makes everything worse. Janaina was a great activist for the rights of transvestites. I was sincerely proud of being a transvestite.
> 
> Dimitri Sales highlights Janaína's legacy: “I had good humor and courage. I had strength!"
> 
> He had good humor and courage. I had strength! After so many struggles, the one he fought for came to mind so that at his graduation he would be called by his social name. When society's eyes only saw transvestites as prostitutes, Janaina became a pedagogue. What a sadness, what a loss… what a time! No rest in peace… celebrate your new life, Jana!”, wrote the Conquistador Dimitri Sales. Through Social Networks, friends inform that Janaína died at home as a victim of a heart attack.



> ANTRA'S NOTE IN Mourning FOR JANAÍNA LIMA
> 
> We lost Janaína Lima, one of the biggest trans activists in Brazil.
> 
> Transvestite, educator and sex professional, was tireless in the fight for citizenship and defense of LGBTI rights +. Born in the Rio Grande do Norte currency with Paraíba, she acted as the assistant coordinator of Policies for LGBTI of São Paulo.
> 
> ANTRA deeply regrets your loss and we wish our deepest condolences to friends and family. Janaina contributes directly to great achievements and was part of ANTRA.
> 
> "For me, transvestility is about freedom. It's being so free to say in this bigoted society that I'm a transvestite." Janaína Lima
> 
> Rest in Power dear Janaína Lima.
> 
> Thank you for everything!!
> 
> We will continue in mourning, in the fight!
