> Michelle Suárez died, the first trans person to occupy a seat in the Uruguayan parliament
> April 25, 2022  Drafting
> The lawyer Michelle Suárez, recognized for having been the first trans person to hold a seat in the Uruguayan Parliament, has died at the age of 38.
> 
> According to local media reported by nearby sources, the former legislator, who was admitted to a hospital, died of a heart problem.
> 
> Suárez, who became the first trans woman to receive a university degree in the country, also worked as a social activist and member of the collective for the defense of the rights of the LGBTIQ community Ovejas Negras.
> 
> From her dual role as lawyer and activist, she was one of the drafters of the Equal Marriage Law project, approved in Uruguay in April 2013, under the mandate of former President José Mujica (2010-2015).
> 
> After being associated with the Communist Party, one of the political forces that make up the left-wing coalition that governed the country between 2005 and 2020, the Broad Front (FA) took a seat in the Senate in October 2017, during the second administration of the late former president Tabaré Vázquez (2005-2010 and 2015-2020).
> 
> Her passage through the parliamentary sphere was marked by a resounding vindication of the importance of more people in his community having access to decision-making positions.
> 
> Suárez, who, upon becoming a senator, said she was  " optimistic "  about advancing the rights agenda for the trans population in the country and aspired to be one of the promoters of the project that in 2018 would become the Comprehensive Law for Trans People of Uruguay, had a brief passage through Parliament.
> 
> Two months after assuming the bench, the then senator submitted her resignation after the complaint that, in her activity as a lawyer, during a legal dispute, she had forged a signature.
> 
> After being sentenced in 2019 to two years of house arrest and another two under supervised release for crimes of forgery of documents, fraud and false testimony, Suárez was disqualified from exercising law and definitively moved away from politics.
> 
> Despite the fact that there were almost no messages of tribute from social groups, after learning of her death, the name of the former senator was a trend on the social network Twitter within Uruguay.
> 
> While many messages alluded to his conviction and attacked his gender identity, others, with phrases such as  "we all have the right to make mistakes,"  recognized the contribution of someone who knew how to be a benchmark in the fight for the rights of the LGBT community in the country.
