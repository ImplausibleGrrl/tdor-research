> Xalapa, Ver.- After several hours of legal procedures, requests and demonstrations, the authorities of the State Attorney General's Office decided to deliver to the members of the LGBTQ+ community the body of Karina, a trans woman who died last Friday at her home in Xalapa.
> 
> In a small display they let the population know that they will give a Christian burial to the trans woman with whom they shared several years of their lives and whose family were their co-workers and friends.
> 
> Read more: Members of the LGBTQ+ community demonstrate, ask that the Prosecutor's Office hand over the body of a trans woman
> 
> "Human rights are for everyone, the value of the family is everything even if it is not blood, coexistence makes you part of it and you can make decisions about it for any member of your circle. For this reason, today, after days of waiting, He did justice and the body of our partner is handed over to be able to give her a Christian burial" , they explained.
> 
> This morning the members of that community demonstrated by blocking Úrsulo Galván street and the offices of the State Attorney General's Office located on Manuel Ávila Camacho avenue, to request that Karina's body be handed over to them, since she has no relatives who They will claim it.
> 
> At the voice of "her family is us" , the members of the community pointed out that Karina worked in the red zone of Xalapa and lost her life due to suffocation while she was at home, according to the expert report. He had previously undergone a tracheotomy, so his death was due to natural causes.
> 
> They mentioned that he died last Friday, the day from which the authorities had been asked to hand over his body so that he could receive a Christian burial.
> 
> They explained that he came from Chiapas to Xalapa when he was 12 years old. At that time he was a child and it was in the capital of Veracruz that he changed his identity to a trans woman.
> 
> “He has no relatives in Xalapa or near the city, his documentation is a bit altered because he is not from Veracruz, he is from Chiapas, when he had the opportunity they issued a birth certificate from Ixhuacán de los Reyes, an investigation has already been carried out and She has no relatives there, she arrived in Xalapa at the age of 12, she arrived as a child and now that she has died at the age of 40, she is a transsexual woman who works as a sex worker on Red Avenue,” they mentioned.
> 
> This situation was explained to the authorities, who were told that no family member would claim her body, so those who shared more than 20 years with her would be in charge of carrying out the corresponding procedures.
> 
> After the demonstrations and the written requests, as well as public requests, the authorities decided to hand over the body, which is why they thanked the support of all the people who collaborated to achieve the task.



> Úrsulo Galván closes to demand the body of Karina López from the Prosecutor's Office
> Xalapa Ver.- (AVC/Verónica Huerta) Úrsulo Galván street was closed by members of "Veracruz Pride" to demand that the authorities of the State Attorney General's Office hand over the body of the transsexual woman Karina López, before sending it to the Common pit.
> 
> The vice president of Orgullo Veracruzano, Eduardo Medina, reported that Karina López died on Friday afternoon in Xalapa, and is under the protection of the FGE Expert Services department, but 5 days have passed, and although they have claimed the body to give Christian burial, the authorities refuse to deliver it.
> 
> Accompanied by the president of Orgullo Veracruzano, Dulce Meléndez, the vice president stated that Karina López worked as a sex worker in "the red zone" of Xalapa, her family is in the state of Chiapas, and she had a birth certificate issued by the Civil Registry. of the municipality of Ixhuacán de Los Reyes.
> 
> “An investigation has already been done, she has no relatives in Ixhuacán; she came to Xalapa at the age of 12 as a child, now that she died at the age of 40 she is a transsexual woman, a sex worker on the red avenue (Lázaro Cárdenas in Xalapa), the point is that she died on Friday and there is no response from the FGE , we already explained to them that there is no family, only us”, reported Eduardo Medina.
> 
> He said that various groups from the Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transsexual, Transgender, Transvestite and Intersex (LGBTTTI) community came together to demand that the FGE hand over Karina's body, because she lived with them for the last 25 years and the adopted as his family, in addition to the fact that no one from Chiapas will claim his remains.
> 
> “They don't want to give them to us. We know that there is a protocol to follow, but it has already been explained to them: They will not find other relatives. If she arrived when she was 12 years old, we have no idea who her family is, we don't want to profit, we just want to give her a Christian burial, give her respect, we have known her for 25 years,” said the leader.
> 
> He regretted that Karina was found dead in her apartment in the Revolution neighborhood, and the experts from the Directorate of Expert Services determined that the cause of death was suffocation, due to a health problem in the trachea.
> 
> Even the plurinominal local deputy of Morena, Iván Chincoya Durán, intervened with the FGE so that they hand over the body, but the authorities refused, and they are delaying it.
> 
> “They are asking us for a relative from Ixhuacan to come, but there is no relative, their only family is us and it belongs to us, they want to do an investigation but they are not going to find anything, there is no family, that is why we all came to ask that they hand it over to us. , she was a human life” they concluded.



> One block from the Government Palace, members of the sexually diverse community protested and closed down Úrsulo Galván street to demand that the Comprehensive Justice Prosecution Unit of the State Attorney General's Office (FGE) hand over the body of a transsexual person. who passed away on January 14.
> 
> According to Eduardo Medina, vice president of the Orgullo Xalapa civil association, the request is to recover the remains of Karina, a 40-year-old person who died in her room of suffocation, presumably due to respiratory problems.
> 
> The protesters commented that she is originally from the state of Chiapas, that she has no relatives in the state capital to claim her body, and therefore, they fear that Karina's remains will end up in a mass grave when they too "are her family."
> 
> Eduardo Medina charged that FGE authorities refuse to hand over the remains of his partner, despite the fact that they have submitted a formal request to the prosecutor, Rafael Aguilar Sosa.
> 
> "The argument given by the Prosecutor's Office is that the body must be claimed by her relatives, but we already told them that no matter how hard they look they will not find it and we are going to be responsible for burying her," he revealed.
> 
> Meanwhile, Karina's body remains under the protection of the General Directorate of Expert Services, whose headquarters are in the Nuevo Xalapa housing unit.
> 
> In the center of the city, his acquaintances and friends also blocked the back of the offices of the Comprehensive Justice Prosecution Unit of the State Attorney General's Office, located on Manuel Ávila Camacho Avenue, where they were later received to attend to his request.







> 19 January, 2022
> Karina Lopez Veracruz
> Members of the Casa de las Muñecas Tiresias AC collective asked the Veracruz authorities to hand over the body of Karina López , a victim of transfeminicide.
> 
> Karina, a trans woman, was found dead since last January 17 , without the Xalapa Attorney General's Office delivering the body for burial.
> 
> “We demand the body as soon as possible to give our sister Karina López a dignified burial,” they indicated.
> 
> Through a statement, Casa de las Muñecas explained that the unit seeks to make them wait at least 15 more days, which is considered inhumane and violates the human rights of the victim.
> 
> READ ALSO: Justice for Natalia: trans activist denounces aggression in Tlalpan hotel
> 
> The collective's representative, Kenya Cuevas, called on the Veracruz State Attorney General's Office to intervene in Karina's case.
> 
> “ We call on the Veracruz Attorney General's Office requesting its intervention to promptly fulfill this important objective of providing humanitarian aid to our trans sisters, especially those who, like Karina herself, live in a situation of vulnerability and without support networks. that they can give him a decent burial ”, he indicated.
> 
> Casa de las Muñecas Tiresias is an association that, among other actions, recovers the bodies of trans women who have died in situations of vulnerability and who do not have a support network.



> Newsroom/Xalapa . Friends of Karina López and the LGBT community of Xalapa asked the Xalapa city council authorities for support in handing over the body of the deceased, since five days have passed since her death and the situation has not been resolved.
> 
> In the statement released for the request for support, it is alluded that "Many travestis and trans people suffer from the abandonment of their families, who suffer discrimination and rejection so that their friends become their new family"
> 
> In the same way, the request for support was made for the mayor of Xalapa Ricardo Ahued.
