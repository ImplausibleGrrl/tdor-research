Kelly Keyze was shot in Caeté, Minas Gerais (Brazil) on 17 Apr 2022.

She was 32.



> The Military Police arrested, on Sunday afternoon (17), a man suspected of having killed a trans woman with 11 shots during the night. Two weapons and drugs were seized from the suspect.
> 
> Kelly Keyze Rosa da Silva, 32, was killed with 11 shots while inside a bar. She was in the company of a brother when she was surprised by six people who emerged from a forest.
> 
> The suspect in the shooting is Tiago Ferreira dos Santos, better known as Tiaguinho, 19 years old. He was taken to the police station on duty, in the Nações Unidas neighborhood, in Sabará.
> 
> The crime took place in the Emboabas neighborhood, in Caeté, in Greater Belo Horizonte. CCTV footage helped arrest the suspect. The motive has not yet been disclosed.
> 
> According to police, the suspect entered the bar with a gun and shot the victim in the back, neck, chest, buttocks and arms. The Military Police are still trying to locate the other suspects to arrest them.
> 
> The victim's brother told the police that they were at the establishment drinking beer when suddenly Tiaguinho and five other individuals came out of a forest in front of the bar. Also according to Kelly's brother, Tiaguinho approached the victim from behind and shot her several times. The victim's body was taken to the IML.

> A trans woman identified as Kelly Keyze, 32, was killed in the early hours of this Sunday (17/4) in Caeté, in the Metropolitan Region of Belo Horizonte (MG).
> 
> According to the Minas Gerais Military Police (PMMG), Kelly was in a bar in the Esmeraldas neighborhood when the suspect, a 19-year-old man, came out of a bush in front of the establishment accompanied by five men.
> 
> Armed, he fired 11 shots into the victim's back, neck, chest, buttocks and arm. The police are still looking for him and the men who accompanied him.
> 
> The motive for the crime has not yet been informed, as the occurrence has not yet been finalized by the Military Police.
> 
> The Civil Police of Minas Gerais (PCMG) informed Metrópoles that, as soon as it was triggered, it moved a criminal investigation to the scene of the facts to identify and collect traces that could collaborate in the investigation. A police investigator also attended to carry out the first surveys.
