> On the first day of 2022, a hate crime was registered in the capital of Puebla. A transgender woman was murdered with extreme violence inside her home, in the El Girasol subdivision.
> 
> Although the body was located last Saturday, January 1, it was until this day that information about it began to emerge.
> 
> Reports indicated that everything happened at approximately 1:30 p.m., when the security guard of the subdivision noticed that an address located at Privada 59 A Poniente and 7 a Sur had the door open.
> 
> The man asked if anyone was inside, but no one answered. Because the door had been open for some time, he requested support from the Municipal Police. 
> 
> They arrived at the place in uniforms from the Secretariat of Citizen Security and upon entering they found the woman's body with signs of violence. Her hands and feet were tied and her head was covered with pants. 
> 
> The cause of death is presumed to be asphyxia by strangulation. 
> 
> The home was cordoned off and the intervention of the State Attorney General's Office was requested to carry out the corresponding proceedings. 
> 
> In an interview with  PÁGINA NEGRA  Gabriela Chumacero, president of the Transgender Group in Puebla, confirmed the finding and denounced that the authorities have not provided information in this regard, so she will seek to meet with the head of the Specialized Unit for the LGBTTTIQ community.
