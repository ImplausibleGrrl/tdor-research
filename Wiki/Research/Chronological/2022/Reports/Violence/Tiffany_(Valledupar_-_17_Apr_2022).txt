Tiffany Landino was stabbed in Valledupar, Cesar (Colombia) on 17 Apr 2022.

She was 22.



> Out of envy they would have murdered a member of the LGBTI community in Valledupar
> Apr 17, 2022 Administrator General
> 
> An alleged dispute over control of the territory where he offered sexual services, at 2:30 am on Sunday, a member of the LGBTI community was murdered in the municipality of Valledupar, specifically in the vicinity of the Public Market.
> 
> The victim was identified as Alejandro José Landino, 22, known as 'Tiffany', who was stabbed in the chest by another member of the LGBTI community, according to the first versions of the events.
> 
> 'Tiffany', who was a Venezuelan national with little time in the city of Valledupar, apparently was working in the sector and was chased by the aggressor, from whom he tried to save himself, but it was impossible. He was immediately transferred to the Santa Isabel clinic, where he died due to the complexity of the stab wound.
> 
> "The victim offered escort services in the popular market sector, and was aware of personal problems with another person from the LGBTI community, because she was new to the sector where she offered such services," the police report indicates.
> 
> Yuliana Ángel, legal representative of Trans Women and Sex Workers in Cesar, stated that lately acts of intolerance have been occurring frequently in Valledupar, so they seek to work in coordination with municipal administrations to protect the population.
> 
> Unfortunately, the attacks on this population are recurrent and the authorities do not take action on the matter, mainly considering that the majority are migrants and there are no care policies for them.
> 
> "We have put a magnifying glass on the Mayor's Office of Valledupar, so that it works in an area of ​​tolerance, and these women are of migrant quality, but we have not been able to advance, they have turned their backs on us and there are no alternatives for them, who daily they are exposed in places of high danger,” he said.
> 
> It is not the first time that a situation like this has occurred, the attacks on the trans population are constant, to the point of being the ones defending themselves due to the little attention they receive from the authorities.
> 
> Regarding the "Tiffany" case, he said he was completely unaware of the alleged dispute he had with another sex worker. "This case is not unique in Valledupar, there is even information that there are groups that extort money and charge for locating a certain social worker in the most frequented areas of the city, but that must be investigated by the Prosecutor's Office," she said.
> 
> According to the versions that handle the case, a taxi driver was the one who alerted about what happened, since he arrived at the Cacique de Upar hotel room, looking for "Tiffany's" sentimental partner, who immediately transferred him to the health center, where unfortunately it was already lifeless.



> A trans woman of Venezuelan origin is murdered in Valledupar
> The victim was 25 years old and worked as a sex worker. It is being investigated whether she was murdered by another person from the LGBTI community.
>
> The group  Caribe afirmativo condemned the murder of Tiffany, atrans womanthat she was a sex worker and that she was murdered in the last few hours with a knife in the city of Valledupar.
> 
> The trans woman spent several months living in the city of Medellín, but on her return to the capital of Cesar she was murdered by a subject whose identity is unknown.
> 
> "Tiffany was killed with stab wounds, she had been in Colombia for several months, she had initially been in Valledupar and then went to Medellín," said Wilson Castañeda, director of Caribe affirmative.
> 
> Tiffany was 25 years old and is the sixth homicide victim of LGBTI members so far this year on the north Colombian coast, according to Caribe affirmative.
