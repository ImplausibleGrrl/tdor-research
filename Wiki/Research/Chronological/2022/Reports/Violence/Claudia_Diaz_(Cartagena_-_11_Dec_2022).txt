> "Another lifeless trans woman": impact in Cartagena for the murder of a beloved hairdresser and leader
> Photo: Reference/Facebook
> Photo: Reference/Facebook
> Monday, December 12, 202209:37 hs
> Impact generated in the commune of Cartagena, Valparaíso Region, the crime of Claudia Díaz (73), renowned trans hairdresser and social leader.
> 
> According to the first records, the woman's body was found by close friends and would present blows to the head.
> 
> According to the San Antonio Leader , the older adult was recognized for her collaboration as an activist for trans women. In the spa city, she worked at the "Claudita Unisex" salon located on Mariano Casanova street with San Antonio.
> 
> “That is how cruel life is, how unfair. We were with her the other day, preparing her birthday for this Wednesday and maybe we are burying her that same day, ”lamented a friend of hers, in dialogue with the aforementioned local media.
> 
> At the moment, the Homicide Brigade is investigating the causes of death and the possible responsibility of third parties.
> 
> THEY ASK FOR JUSTICE
> Aware of this crime, Deputy Emilia Schneider stressed the urgency of investigating this and other "hate crimes."
> 
> “They find another lifeless trans woman, in the Valparaíso Region, which has already been classified as a red zone for the LGBTI+ population due to hate crimes. It is urgent to investigate the death of Claudia Diaz and many others, and also to provide in-depth answers against discrimination and hatred," the trans parliamentarian wrote on a Twitter account.
