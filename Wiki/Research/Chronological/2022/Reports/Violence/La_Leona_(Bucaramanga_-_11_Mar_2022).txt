La Leona was stabbed in a beauty salon in Bucaramanga, Santander (Colombia) on or shortly before 11 Mar 2022.


> “How many trans women should be killed in Bucaramanga to change this situation of violence?” asked the NGO Proyecto VIHDA Santander after the murder of La Leona , a 29-year-old transgender woman who was found dead inside a beauty salon located in Bucaramanga. in carrera 18 with carrera 30 of the capital of Santander. According to the first versions, two men entered her establishment and stabbed her in the neck. They then fled on a motorcycle.
> 
> Although they tried to take her to a health center, the depth of the wound took her life at the time of the attack. The Police reported that they are reviewing security cameras to identify those responsible for the murder of this woman, a native of Norte de Santander.
> 
> According to data from the Fundación Grupo de Acción y Apoyo Trans (GAAT), with the death of La Leona there would be five trans women murdered in 2022. “2021 showed alarming records with an increase trend of 25% with 36 deaths registered," the organization said.



> The LGBT community denounced during the last hours the murder of the trans activist known as La Leona, whose lifeless body was found in the facilities of a hairdresser.
> 
> The violent events took place in an aesthetic services establishment located in the Colombian municipality of Bucaramanga, when those responsible, not yet identified, attacked the woman with a knife in the neck, an attack that effectively ended the life of the victim. .
> 
> Without a doubt, the event has shaken the emotions of the community, as one more is added to their losses in the course of 2022, before which they demand justice so that the authorities find the actors of such a heinous crime. In addition, they also complain to the Government about the lack of guarantees to protect the lives of trans people.
> 
> “Trans women survive daily in the midst of indifference, hatred and oblivion. Today La Leona was murdered in the Center of #Bucaramanga . They are in serious danger, we demand justice and actions to protect them and in turn meet demands that continue to be ignored, ”activist Olga Materón said through her twitter account.
