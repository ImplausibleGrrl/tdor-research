> Security in Jalisco: They arrest the alleged murderer of a trans woman in Tlajomulco
> The detainee is Eric Brandon "S", who is already at the disposal of the judge who requested it
> 
> August 24, 2022 - 6:28 p.m.
> 
> Thanks to inquiries by the Specialized Unit for Attention to Crimes due to Sexual Diversity of the Special Prosecutor for Human Rights (FEDH), it was possible to identify the probable person responsible for the death of a trans woman, who has already been apprehended. The detainee is Eric Brandon "S", who is already available to the judge who requested it.
> 
> From the investigation folder, it emerged that the victim was taken by relatives to receive medical attention at a Tlajomulco de Zúñiga aid station on January 11 of this year at approximately 8:10 p.m., since she had been injured by bullets ; the affected one, when being treated by doctors, no longer had vital signs.
> 
> The Specialized Unit for Attention to Crimes due to Sexual Diversity of the Special Prosecutor for Human Rights carried out the investigative acts with a differential and specialized approach. It was thus that Eric Brandon was identified as likely responsible for the crime. As there was sufficient evidence, the agent of the Public Ministry requested the arrest warrant.

> A man of approximately 25 years of age was shot on Villa la Palma street in the Villa Fontana Aqua subdivision, in the municipality of Tlajomulco de Zúñiga, he was taken to a hospital where he died.
> 
> Neighbors in the area heard several detonations of a firearm, when leaving, they observed a man lying and unconscious, so they called 911 to request help from authorities.
> 
> Paramedics arrived at the site who attended and transferred the subject to Cruz Verde Las Conchas, moments later he died despite prompt medical attention. According to the authorities, the man received at least three bullet wounds.
> 
> No further details were obtained about him or the perpetrators and it was the staff of the State Prosecutor's Office who opened an investigation folder to find the whereabouts of the aggressors.
> 
> Finally, elements of the Jalisco Institute of Forensic Sciences transferred the body to their facilities to perform the autopsy of law.
