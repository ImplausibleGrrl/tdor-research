> The Supreme Court has allowed bail application of a co-accused in the murder case of a transgender reported in Nagpur in July 2019. The division bench of Justices Ravindra Bhatt and Dipankar Datta granted bail to accused Sheikh Nisar Sheikh.
> 
> The bail application was rejected by the Nagpur Bench of Bombay High Court on July 15, 2021. The applicant is co-accused in the murder case of transgender Pravin Gajbhiye alias Chamcham who was brutally attacked and killed by her former mentor-turned-rival Uttam Bawa Senapati and her gang at Kamna Nagar in Kalamna in July 2019.
> 
> The petitioner was accused No. 5 in the case registered under Sections 302, 307, 341, 323, 143, 147, 148, 149 of IPC read with Sections 4/25 of Indian Arms Act and Section 135 of Maharashtra Police Act.
> 
> The counsel for the petitioner submitted in the court that the petitioner has already undergone imprisonment for more than three years. The case of the prosecution was that Chamcham was assaulted by Uttam baba Guru, Kiran Gawali, Chattu Kamal Uikey, Nishar Sheikh and two unknown persons with knife and sticks and killed her.
> 
> The counsel for the accused pointed out before the court that the witness did not point out the prominent role of this accused in a statement recorded under Section 164 of CrPC. After considering all the submissions, the Apex court granted bail to the accused. Advs Ramesh Rawlani and Atul Rawlani represented the petitioner.



> Assaulted transgender activist succumbs
> TNN / Jun 11, 2019, 04:46 IST
> Assaulted transgender activist succumbs
> Nagpur: Transgender activist Pravin Gajbhiye alias Chamcham, brutally assaulted with sharp weapons on June 4, was declared dead at New Era hospital on Monday where she had been fighting for life. Chamcham was attacked by her former mentor-turned-rival Uttam Bawa Senapati and her gang at Kamna Nagar in Kalamna. Police have now slapped murder charge on Senapati and others. Chamcham, who had earlier floated a political party and also contested civic elections, was attacked by Senapati at latter’s home following an ongoing tussle over distribution of area, switching loyalty of each others’ followers and sharing of collections and donations. Senapati too had floated a political outfit ‘Baliraja party’. Following assault, Kalamna police arrested Senapati, her close aide Kiran Gawli, Kamal Uikey, Sonu Parseonikar and Shakir Shaikh. Kalamna police had procured the custody remand of five till Monday. The court had sent Shaikh, Parseonikar and Uikey to jail. Senapati and Kiran were admitted at Mayo hospital after police wanted to conduct a gender examination test on them. The post-mortem of Chamcham would be conducted amid tight security at Mayo hospital.
