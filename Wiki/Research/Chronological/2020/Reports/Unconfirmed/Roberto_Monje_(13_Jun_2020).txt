A 40-year-old man was stabbed to death in his house in the Santa Fe city of Reconquista, and after the fact, a group that works for the rights of sexual minorities denounced that it is a "hate crime," sources reported Sunday. judicial.

The victim was identified by Justice as Roberto Monje, who resided in a house located on 1020 Ludueña Street, in said city in the northeast of the province of Santa Fe.

Judicial sources informed Télam that the stabbed body was found yesterday at his home after the domestic worker arrived at the scene and did not obtain any responses from the man.

Given this situation, the woman went to a shop in the area where Monje worked and asked her owners if they knew anything about her employee, to which they replied that they had not heard from him since Friday.

According to sources, these people decided to alert the Police, who soon after moved to the victim's house, where he warned that the door was open and its inhabitant lay dead in the bathroom, with several stab wounds to his body.

After the discovery, prosecutor Alejandro Rodríguez, from the Public Prosecution Office (MPA), intervened, who ordered the corresponding autopsy and a series of expert reports at the home where a broken glass and traces of blood were found.

For its part, the Vanesa Zabala Front, which bears that name in tribute to a transvestite murdered years ago, said in a statement that "Reconquest is once again the site of a hate crime, in the midst of an ultra-conservative advance against sexual dissent. "

"Those of us who met Robert for his activism in defense of the rights of sexual dissent, find ourselves faced with painful news and a situation that makes clear the continued existence of hate crimes," the text added.

He then pointed out that "as happened with the transvesticide of Vanesa Zabala, we call on the community and the social and political organizations to fight and demand clarification and justice in the face of such a crime."
