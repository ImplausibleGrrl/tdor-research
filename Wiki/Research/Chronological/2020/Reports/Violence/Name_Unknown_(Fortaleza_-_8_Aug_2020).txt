A young transvestite was shot to death on Saturday night (8), at Dallas Street, in the Granja Lisboa neighborhood, in Fortaleza. According to information from the Public Security Secretariat (SSPDS), the victim, unidentified so far, was killed by gunfire.

People who live near the crime scene say they saw the teenager entering a thicket minutes before the shots were heard. She was about 15 years old.


A transvestite of just 15 years old, was shot to death on Saturday night (8), in the neighborhood of Granja Lisboa, in Fortaleza.

The victim was reportedly lured to a vacant lot and executed by two men. There was so much fear at the scene that residents did not even call an ambulance after the transvestite was shot. It was the police who called the medical team to try to save the victim's life.

The ambulance took about 30 minutes to reach the site and this may have decreased the chances of survival. As she was not carrying documents and her family members were not at the scene, the police did not provide the name of the transvestite. The suspects fled on foot. The police believe the death is related to drug debt to drug dealers.

According to the Secretariat of Public Security and Social Defense (SSPDS), the Department of Homicide and Personal Protection (DHPP) of the Civil Police of the State of Ceará (PCCE) investigates the homicide and that the victim would be a transvestite.

The Forensic Forensic of the State of Ceará (Pefoce) also attended the place of the occurrence, where it collected evidence that will assist the police work. A police inquiry was initiated by the DHPP, which carries out investigations in order to identify the suspects, as well as to discover the motivation of the fact.
