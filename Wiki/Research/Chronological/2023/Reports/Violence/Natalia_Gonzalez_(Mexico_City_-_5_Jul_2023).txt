> Mexico City, Jul 6 (EFE).- Mexican social organizations denounced this Thursday the transfemicide of Natalia González, who was found murdered on Wednesday night in a hotel in Mexico City, while demanding justice for the crime.
> 
> "Yesterday, Wednesday, July 5, 2023, Natalia González Santiago, a 29-year-old trans woman and sex worker, was the victim of a violent murder at the Condesa hotel, located in Tlalpan, Mexico City," said the organization Casa of the Tiresias Dolls.
> 
> In a publication on their social networks, the citizen group explained that the woman's body appeared in a room of said hotel "showing blood stains and a knife on her left side."
> 
> He also explained that at the scene, Mexican authorities arrested Axel "N", who allegedly murdered Natalia, but the defendant claimed to have acted in self-defense.
> 
> The organization demanded justice in the case and raised its voice "so that the murder of our sister Natalia does not go unpunished."
> 
> "We urge the authorities to carry out an exhaustive and transparent investigation to clarify all the details of this crime, ensuring that due process is applied and that the culprit is brought to justice," he stressed.



> Natalia González, a 29-year-old trans woman, was brutally stabbed to death on the night of July 5 in a hotel located in the Benito Juárez mayor's office , in Mexico City . The events shocked the LGBT+ community and society.
> 
> After receiving the incident report, agents from the Mexico City Citizen Security Secretariat went to the scene of the crime and managed to arrest the alleged attacker, who confessed to having stabbed Natalia González with a knife. According to reporter Carlos Jiménez, the subject alleged that he acted in "self-defense" , arguing that she had attacked him first.
> 
> The Mexico City Prosecutor's Office has already started an investigation into this cruel murder.
> 
> The LGBT+ community and human rights defenders expressed their outrage and demanded justice for Natalia, as well as forceful actions to combat the violence and discrimination faced by transgender people in the country.
