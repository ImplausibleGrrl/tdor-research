> Trans woman stabbed to death in Rio de Janeiro
> 
> The nursing technician, Julia Nicolly Moreira da Silva, 34, was buried this Thursday (27) after being found dead at her home in Belford Roxo, in the Baixada Fluminense of Rio de Janeiro, last Tuesday (25) ). The woman, who worked for the Social Organization (OS) Ideias and worked at the Getúlio Vargas Filho Hospital, in Niterói, and at the Women's Hospital in São João de Meriti, was gagged and stabbed to death.
> 
> According to the report by Antra (National Association of Transvestites and Transsexuals), which analyzed data from 2022, Brazil is the country with the most deaths of trans and transvestites in the world for the 14th consecutive year. For the family, Julia was the victim of a hate crime, motivated by transphobia.
> 
> According to family members, the nursing technician was hit by two men, with several stab wounds. The case is being investigated by the Civil Police. Julia had been the target of violent attacks before. In 2021, a man stabbed her. At the time, he was arrested red-handed and convicted of attempted feminicide for a vile reason, unable to defend the victim.
> 
> Antra's report points out that 131 trans and transvestite people were murdered in the country in 2022, and 20 people took their own lives in the face of discrimination and prejudice present in Brazilian society.
> 
> Data on the murder of trans people in Brazil remain high. The National Association of Transvestites and Transsexuals released a partial figure of 46 murders of trans and transvestites between January 1st and April 30th of this year. Among the victims, 45 were trans women and transvestites, and 1 trans man.
