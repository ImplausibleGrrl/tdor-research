> Police arrest suspects of killing trans woman in Ubá, Minas Gerais 
> Another trans woman is appointed as the mastermind; Human bones found by the police could be those of a victim
> By Larissa Ricci
> 
> 02/09/2023 at 09:17 am
>
> Police arrest suspects of killing trans woman in Ubá, Minas Gerais 
> The victim had been missing since January 4
> Two people, aged 28 and 29, were arrested on suspicion of killing and hiding the body of another 25-year-old trans woman in Ubá, in the Zona da Mata. The victim had been missing since January 4. The information was released by the Civil Police of Minas Gerais (PCMG) this Wednesday (8).
> 
> Police surveys indicated that a trans woman, aged 28, ordered the crime and her boyfriend, aged 29, the executor.
> 
> According to the corporation, the crime would have happened out of revenge on behalf of a witch in November last year.
> 
> Last Tuesday (7), a human skeleton was located in a stream in the neighboring municipality, Tocantins. Genetic materials, including those from family members, are being collected and will be sent to the Legal Medical Institute of Belo Horizonte for examination.
> 
> "In the place where we found the bones, the clothes that the victim was wearing when he was last seen were located. In addition, a slipper, documents and a cell phone that belonged to her were also found", informed the delegate Douglas Motta.
> 
> On the day of the crime
> 
> Investigations pointed out that the victim was last seen getting inside the man's vehicle, at Praça Getúlio Vargas, in Ubá, heading towards the city of Tocantins. She would have been executed with a shot to the head.
> 
> Those investigated were sent to the prison system and remain at the disposal of Justice


> The human skeleton found by teams of the Civil Police of Minas Gerais (PCMG) this Tuesday (7), in a stream in the municipality of Tocantins, in the Zona da Mata, may be that of a 25-year-old trans woman, who disappeared in Ubá , in the same region, on January 4th.
> 
> Police investigations resulted in the temporary arrest of a 28-year-old trans woman, 28, and her 29-year-old boyfriend, suspected of aggravated homicide and concealment of a corpse. In a press conference granted to the press this Wednesday (8), PCMG delegates gave details of the murder.
> 
> According to them, genetic samples collected from the remains found in the stream, during searches carried out to collect materials of interest to the investigation, will be forwarded to Instituto Médico Legal Dr. André Roquette, PCMG unit in Belo Horizonte. 
> 
> "In the place where we found the bones, the clothes that the victim was wearing when he was last seen were located. In addition, a slipper, documents and a cell phone that belonged to her were also found", informed the delegate Douglas Motta.
> 
> Experts will also analyze genetic material collected from relatives, for DNA comparison. Those investigated were sent to the prison system, where they remain at the disposal of Justice.
> 
> During the press conference, Chief Douglas Motta explained that, as soon as the PCMG became aware of the disappearance, steps were taken to try to locate the victim.
> 
> "During the investigations, it was found that the trans woman had been murdered", said the police chief, who also highlighted that PCMG surveys showed that a 28-year-old trans woman is suspected of being the mastermind of the murder and her 29-year-old boyfriend the executor of the crime.
> 
> Investigations by the Civil Police indicated that the victim was last seen getting inside the man's vehicle, at Praça Getúlio Vargas, in Ubá, heading towards the city of Tocantins. Preliminary investigations indicate that the victim would have been executed with a firearm shot in the frontal region of the head.
> 
> The crime would have been motivated by revenge, due to a previous disagreement, in November last year, when there was a fight between the victim and the investigated.




> The Civil Police of Minas Gerais (PCMG) clarified the death of a 25-year-old trans woman, who had been missing since January 4, in the municipality of Ubá, in the Zona da Mata region of Minas Gerais. After investigative work, on Tuesday (7/2), two suspects, aged 28 and 29, were temporarily arrested for the practice of qualified homicide and concealment of a corpse.
> 
> During searches carried out to collect materials of interest to the investigation, a human skeleton, supposedly of the victim, was also located, yesterday, in a stream in the neighboring municipality, Tocantins. Genetic materials, including those from family members, are being collected and will be forwarded to Instituto Médico Legal Dr. André Roquette, PCMG unit in Belo Horizonte, to carry out a DNA comparison test.
> 
> “In the place where we found the bones, the clothes that the victim was wearing when he was last seen were located. In addition, a slipper, documents and a cell phone that belonged to her were also found,” said Deputy Douglas Motta.
> 
> Those investigated were sent to the prison system and remain at the disposal of Justice.
> 
> During a press conference held this Wednesday (8/2), Douglas Motta spoke about the investigation of the facts. According to him, as soon as the PCMG became aware of the disappearance, steps were taken to try to locate the victim. “During the investigations, it was found that the trans woman had been murdered”, explained the delegate, informing that surveys by the PCMG showed that a 28-year-old trans woman is suspected of being the mastermind of the homicide and her boyfriend, of 29, the executor of the crime.
> 
> Investigations by the Civil Police indicated that the victim was last seen getting inside the man's vehicle, at Praça Getúlio Vargas, in Ubá, heading towards the city of Tocantins. Preliminary investigations indicate that the victim would have been executed with a firearm fired in the frontal region of the head.
> 
> The crime would have happened for reasons of revenge, due to a previous disagreement, in November last year, when there was a fight between the victim and the investigated.
> 
> Investigations continue. “We do not rule out the possibility of a possible participant in this fact”, Douglas pointed out.
